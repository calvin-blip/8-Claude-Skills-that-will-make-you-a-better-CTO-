# 8-Claude-Skills-that-will-make-you-a-better-CTO-
A collection of eight purpose-built Claude skills designed to make you a sharper, faster, and more effective engineering executive. Each skill activates inside Claude as an on-demand specialist covering the leadership and technical challenges CTOs face most.
Built by Nivaro. Engineered for leaders who move fast and build things that last.

How to Install Any Skill

Download the skill folder you want
Zip the folder (make sure the folder itself is the root of the ZIP, not just the file inside)
Go to Claude and navigate to Customize > Skills
Upload the ZIP file
Make sure code execution is enabled
Describe your situation and the skill takes over


The Skills
1. CTO-Negotiation-Coach
High-stakes negotiations are where CTOs lose the most value and the most political capital. Whether you are fighting for budget, pushing back on a vendor contract, or trying to get a stubborn CEO to fund a platform rewrite, this skill coaches you through it using frameworks drawn from FBI negotiation tactics, Harvard's principled negotiation method, and real executive psychology.
You tell it what you are negotiating, who you are up against, and what you need to walk away with. It helps you define your optimum goal versus your fallback, map the other party's pressure points, and select the right tactic: whether that is strategic silence, building a golden bridge, or executing a clean tactical retreat. It will also roleplay as your opponent so you can pressure-test your approach before the real conversation.
What to expect: a customized negotiation playbook and the option to practice the actual conversation before it happens.

2. Technical-Debt-Roadmapper
Most tech debt programs fail because they live in a backlog that never gets prioritized. This skill replaces that broken system with a structured, phased remediation roadmap that integrates cleanup directly into your development lifecycle.
You describe your current state: how you track debt, how much unplanned work is bleeding your velocity, and whether you are dealing with code debt, architecture debt, or cognitive debt. The skill walks you through establishing a visible debt register, applying behavioral code analysis to find your highest-impact hotspots, choosing the right capacity allocation model (the 20% rule, the Shopify 25% model, or ticket-attached cleanup), and building automation pipelines that prevent new debt from accumulating.
What to expect: a 30-60-90 day roadmap with quick wins, allocation frameworks, and prevention infrastructure.

3. ML-Data-Debt-Auditor
Machine learning systems accumulate a category of debt that traditional software frameworks completely miss. This skill is built on Google's ML systems research and ISO/IEC 5338 AI lifecycle standards to help you find and fix the hidden drag inside your AI infrastructure.
You describe your current ML architecture and the skill immediately scans for the most dangerous anti-patterns: pipeline jungles (organically evolved data prep that no one fully understands), glue code bloat (the 95% of ML systems that is not actually doing machine learning), and CACE violations (changes to one feature silently corrupting the behavior of every other feature). It then classifies your data debt into acquisition, quality, and labeling categories and prescribes a concrete remediation roadmap aligned to international AI engineering standards.
What to expect: a structured audit of your ML architecture with specific anti-patterns identified and a standards-backed remediation plan.

4. Cognitive-Debt-AI-Strategist
Your team is shipping AI-generated code. The question is whether anyone actually understands it. Cognitive debt is the new category of technical debt that accumulates when code passes tests and looks clean but no one on the team can explain what it does or why it was built that way.
This skill helps you redesign your PR review process for the age of AI coding tools. It introduces the core review standard: the author must be able to explain the logic without reading it again. It also trains reviewers to spot AI-specific failure modes including architectural mismatches, redundant library introductions, and runtime blindness (code that passes linters but fails silently in production under real concurrency conditions).
What to expect: upgraded PR guidelines, AI-specific review checklists, and a hardened testing strategy for catching what static analysis misses.

5. LLM-Refactoring-Prompt-Engineer
If your team is using AI to refactor code without a structured prompting approach, they are introducing risk they cannot see. This skill teaches engineering teams how to construct prompts that produce safe, behavior-preserving refactoring outputs from models like GPT-4o, DeepSeek, or Claude.
You describe your refactoring goal and the skill helps you build a complete prompt that includes role assignment priming, chain-of-thought reasoning, concrete before-and-after examples as behavioral anchors, explicit output constraints to eliminate hallucinated commentary, and precise scope fencing so the model does not touch what it should not. It also prescribes the validation pipeline: CodeBLEU scoring, cyclomatic complexity comparison, and full unit test execution to confirm semantic preservation before anything merges.
What to expect: a production-ready prompt structure for your refactoring workflow and a validation checklist to prove the code still does what it did before.

6. Amazing-CTO-Coach
The hardest transition in engineering leadership is the moment you have to stop being the best developer in the room and start being the executive who builds the room. This skill is a direct mentorship tool for CTOs and technical founders making that shift.
It coaches you on three fronts. First, automatic management: how to use 1-on-1s to translate business context so deeply that your team makes the right technical decisions without your involvement. Second, tech zoo consolidation: how to enforce a minimal, stable stack without losing your best engineers to boredom. Third, 10x scaling: how to think ahead about what breaks first if your customer base and headcount both multiply by ten, and build a proactive plan before the breaking point arrives.
What to expect: an honest assessment of where you are stuck as a leader and a concrete plan to delegate more, simplify your architecture, and scale your org before it is a crisis.

7. Product-Debt-Lifecycle-Manager
Product debt is not in your code. It is in your product decisions. It is the VIP workflow someone built for a single partner three years ago. It is the custom integration nobody documented. It is every feature that still has to be updated every time you touch the core system, even though no one remembers why it exists.
This skill helps you find that debt before it explodes during a modernization effort. It applies the Chester's Fence protocol: before you delete or consolidate any feature, you must trace its origin and understand why it was built. It also helps you map the landmines inside any planned rewrite so you do not discover forgotten business requirements after you have already thrown away the system that was carrying them.
What to expect: a structured audit of your legacy feature surface, a Chester's Fence investigation protocol, and a phased deprecation or consolidation roadmap.

8. CEO-Expectation-Manager
A significant portion of CTO stress is self-inflicted. It comes from assuming the CEO wants everything faster and cheaper and better simultaneously, without ever having that conversation explicitly. This skill helps you have it.
It coaches you on how to diagnose which corner of the Iron Triangle (fast, good, cheap) is being squeezed and how to force your stakeholders to consciously choose their trade-off instead of demanding all three. It helps you script the 1-on-1 conversation that clears assumed priorities and replaces them with explicitly stated ones. And it helps you design a radical transparency protocol so that when incidents happen, budget is lost, or debt accumulates, you are communicating proactively rather than managing secrets that corrode executive trust over time.
What to expect: a CEO alignment script, an Iron Triangle negotiation framework, and a transparency communication plan you can implement immediately.
